Queen

Brian May, Freddie Mercury, John Deacon, Roger Taylor

Architects of Sound

Behind the "heavy wall of sound" and the moments of "quiet harmony" stand four dreamers who became something much louder than the sum of their parts: Freddie Mercury (vocals and piano), Brian May (guitar), Roger Taylor (drums), and John Deacon (bass guitar). Together, they formed a unique democratic unit where every member was a primary songwriter and a keeper of the eternal flame. We invite you to explore the stories of these four individuals who joined together to create this beautiful, chaotic noise.

Queen is one of the most iconic and influential British rock bands in history, world-renowned for their theatrical performances and genre-blending sound. Led by the incomparable frontman Freddie Mercury, the group created timeless anthems like "Bohemian Rhapsody" and "We Will Rock You" that remain global staples today. The band is celebrated for its incredible musical versatility, combining elements of rock, opera, and pop with the virtuosic guitar work of Brian May and a powerful rhythmic foundation.
